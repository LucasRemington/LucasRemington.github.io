Softbodies

Softbodies was my attempt at making the cutest, simplest mobile game I could manage. Because of its simplicity, I was able to implement key UI elements without much difficulty. The postmortem is available online: however, I did add enviornmental sounds and anti-death spiral mechanics in a recent update. In the future, I plan to introduce an 'infinite mode' to procedurally generate levels, and publish the final release to the android store.
For a video preview, please visit the itch.io page.

Team Size: 1

Development Time: ~3 weeks, early 2018

Role: Sole developer.

Music Credit: TJ Mitchell

Gyro's Escape

An infinite runner originally developed for PC, Gyro's Escape was recently ported over to Android. As a member of the original development team, I was asked to assist with the port, and contributed all 2D assets used in the final game. In it, you play as a robot desperate to escape the facility where he was created, yet also doomed to remain trapped, running, forever,

Guardian's Awakening

Guardian's Awakening is a 2.5d platformer set in a dying world: as a Golem, a supposed guardian of the garden, you traverse through the ruined and spider-infested wreckage of the place you were created to protect, collecting mementos of the past. While my primary contribution to this project was level design, I also all of the dialogue and text popups, and was responsible for organizing and directing my other team members.
For a video preview, please visit the itch.io page.

Team Size: 4

Development Time: ~4 weeks, early 2018

Roles: Level designer, 2d artist, producer, narrative designer.

Team Members: Sam Gomes, Dakota Lyons, and Valeera Davis.

Loot Everything

While I was primarily in a production role on this project, I also faciliated communication between the art team and the engineering team, and was responsible for writing and implementing all writing on the various signs found througout the game.

Echo Hunt

Echo Hunt is a two-player VR game developed over three days at the 2017 Fitchburg State University game jam. One player would assume the role of a 'predator,' while the other took on the role of 'prey.' The 'predator' would, while wearing a VR headset, send out pulses of sound to locate the prey, who would attempt to survive a certain amount of time while scavenging for food, just outside the predators vision. In this project, I was responsible for UX, designing the entire tutorial sequence and implementing various 2D assets as visual aids.
Video footage featuring an explanation of gameplay can be found here.

Team Size: 6

Development Time: 3 days, early 2017

Roles: UX design, 2D artist.

Team Members: Greg Westland, Brett Crowe, Luis Valle, Frankie Gallagher, Daniel Wolshin

JetHack

JetHack is a 3D platformer taking place in a futuristic cityscape, developed over three days at the 2018 Fitchburg State University game jam. The gameplay primarily consists of using a jetpack to fly from building to building, and enter 'code' into hackable terminals to disrupt the insidious corporate marketing being transmitted across the city. In this project, I was primarily responsible for creating numerous 2D elements that were integrated into the environment, most notably the textures affixed to each building.

Team Size: 5

Development Time: 3 days, early 2018

Role: 2D artist.

Team Members: Kenny Howell, Marissa Tautiva, Christopher Cornford, Ethan Thibault
