Biography

A quick summary of my life so far.

Super Short Version: I've done a lot of stuff.

My life, briefly and incompletely summarized by ten facts:

1. Despite living in Massachusetts for as long as I can remember, I still hate seafood.

2. I have been playing and designing games since I was five years old. My first ever piece was a ripoff of Yu-Gi-Oh. Thankfully, I've had other ideas since then.

3. I am not a Chihuahua. However, I do wear an orange hat almost all the time. It's sort of become a trademark fashion choice.

4. My favorite authors are Terry Pratchett and Kurt Vonnegut, for similar reasons - namely, that while their respective narrative styles are very clear and easy to understand, they manage to tackle incredibly complex philosophical and politicial issues while being entertaining.

5. For many years, I attended the Wizards and Warriors LARP camp, even working as a Counselor-in-Training from 2013 to 2014. In 2014, I recieved special recognition for my performance, getting the Monster Camper of the Session award.

6. I'm a big fan of eSports, and follow the pro scenes of several games - notably, League of Legends, Magic the Gathering, Hearthstone, and Overwatch. If you added together all of the hours I spent playing all of those games, you'd end up with way too many hours.

7. I'm currently attending Fitchburg State University as a Game Design major, Psychology minor. One of my pieces was selected to be shown at the schoolwide juried showcase Visions in 2016.

8. Though I am fascinated by all forms of media, I have a particular fondness for tabletop roleplaying games, such as Dungeons and Dragons. I 'Dungeon Master' on the regular, and have worked with my schools Game Design club to host several events like the RPG night, or the Tabletop RPG DMing Talk. I am also designing a game of this sort, as a special independent study with one of my professors and several other students.

9. I'm also very interested in developing VR games. The first time that I put on a VR headset was one of the most incredible and immersive experiences I've ever had - I truly believe that we will come to utilize that technology extensively in future years, and am always looking for opportunities in that field. During the 2016 Game Jam, I designed the UI for one, and my team came in second place out of the eight competing at my venue.

10. I made this website myself, using html. It's my first time ever working with the language, so feel free to contact me and let me know what you think!

Design Philosophies

How I approach creating art.

Super Short Version: I try to create good stuff.

My principles of design:

1. I strive to express some aspect of myself through every piece I create.

2. I try to learn something new from every piece I create, and make every piece as perfect as I possbily can.

3. I show respect to the intelligence of my audience, never putting myself or my work above them.

4. Good art either entertains or enlightens. Great art does both. I strive to create great art, though I do not always succeed.

5. Art can be created in any medium. However, different mediums require different approaches to create the best possible art.

6. What sets games apart from other mediums is the players ability to make meaningful choices.
